Cardio Health

Cadence: It is the number of steps per minute, crucial for sprint speed and efficiency.

Interpretation: Higher cadence means quicker steps, balanced stride length, and better momentum.

Recommendation: Use high-knee drills, strength training, and technique refinement to increase cadence.

Heart Rate: It measures the number of heartbeats per minute, indicating cardiovascular intensity and endurance.

Interpretation: Lower heart rate means a healthier heart, reduced risk of cardiovascular disease.

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy heart rate by exercising regularly and following a balanced diet.

Body Mass Index (BMI)

BMI: BMI is a measure of body fat based on height and weight.

Interpretation: Higher BMI means higher body fat, increased risk of health issues.

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy BMI by exercising regularly and following a balanced diet.

Foot Health

Toe Pressure: It is the force exerted by the toes during foot strike, crucial for push-off power and stability.

Interpretation: Lower toe pressure means a healthy foot, reduced risk of foot issues.

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy foot by exercising regularly and following a balanced diet.

Heel Pressure: It is the force exerted on the heel during foot strike, influencing stability and impact absorption.

Interpretation: Lower heel pressure means a healthy foot, reduced risk of foot issues.

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy foot by exercising regularly and following a balanced diet.

Center of Pressure: The point where force is concentrated on the foot during contact, impacting balance & stability.

Interpretation: Lower center of pressure means a healthy foot, reduced risk of foot issues.

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy foot by exercising regularly and following a balanced diet.

Flat Foot: Flat foot is a low or absent foot arch, affecting alignment and support.

Interpretation: Better gait stability means a healthier body, reduced risk of injury.

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy foot by exercising regularly and following a balanced diet.

Gait Stability

Stride Length: It is the distance covered in one step during a sprint, contributing to overall speed and efficiency.

Interpretation: Better stride length means a healthier body, reduced risk of injury.

Stride Velocity: The rate of change in stride length, indicating the speed of the child's gait.

Interpretation: Better stride velocity means a healthier body, reduced risk of injury.

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy stride velocity by exercising regularly and following a balanced diet.
